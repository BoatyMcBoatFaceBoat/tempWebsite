Model

Science models the world. The creation of a reliable model is essential in approaching complex problems. Our approach is rooted in mathematics. Mathematics invents axioms and rules, but even more importantly a language to discuss its elements. A well chosen and descriptive language is almost the same as a solution to your problem.

We do not have to do this from scratch. There exist a number of modeling frameworks, that help us express relationships. These usually come with graphics, so that the knowledge contained in the model can be visualized for its audience in easily understandable diagrams. For example UML is often used for modeling IT-related issues. We find the IDEF0, though limited with respect to UML, is excellent for things like information flows. At times we have developed problem oriented frameworks to address specific questions.

Mathematics is rigourous in the sense that theorems have irrefutable proofs. In the same way, we aim for models that we can trust to be complete.
